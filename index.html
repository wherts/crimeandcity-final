Visualizing Changing Populations in Echo Park


Wesley Herts

Crime and the City - Final Project

This is a visualization I built for my final project for the course Crime and the City in the Urban Studies Department at Brown University. This project seeks to allow users to understand population changes in Echo Park over the past 16 years (along with some projections). Echo Park is an area of Los Angeles that we studied in the course. It is an area that has recently experience severe gentrification.

Users can investigate data encompassing the population, distribution of housing, and income breakdown for the covered time period. Data is broken down by census tract, the smallest-scale breakdown of data that was available. All the data came from SimplyMap, a data tool that contains US Census data as well as from other sources. It can be accessed through Brown University's Library. Both visualizations are interactive: try mousing over and clicking different pieces to understand the data.
